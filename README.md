# EC633-Econometrics-of-Programme-Evaluation

This paper estimates the effect of the Mahalakshmi Scheme — a Telangana govern-
ment programme providing free public bus travel and a monthly cash transfer of
Rs. 500 to women — on female labour force participation (FLFP), using a difference-
in-differences design that compares women aged 18–59 in Telangana to those in
Andhra Pradesh across twelve quarterly rounds of the Periodic Labour Force Survey
(2022 Q4 – 2025 Q3). The static DiD yields a small and statistically insignificant av-
erage treatment effect, but the dynamic event study reveals a more nuanced picture:
pre-treatment coefficients are jointly insignificant (F (3, 4) = 1.20, p = 0.42), sup-
porting the parallel trends assumption, while post-treatment coefficients are jointly
highly significant (F (4, 4) = 29.30, p = 0.003) and grow increasingly negative over
time, reaching−0.085 percentage points by the eighth post-treatment quarter. I
interpret this finding through the lens of distress-driven female employment: auxil-
iary regressions within Telangana reveal a strong monotonically declining gradient
between household consumption and FLFP, consistent with poorer women partici-
pating out of economic compulsion. The scheme’s income transfer appears to release
women from necessity-driven employment, producing a welfare-improving reduction
in measured participation rather than labour market exclusion.
